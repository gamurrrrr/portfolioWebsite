Executive Summary

The objective of this project was to analyze operational data across four key pillars: Warehouse Efficiency, Inventory Health, Financial Trends, and Seasonal Demand. By analyzing 4 years of historical data, I identified critical cost-saving opportunities for the 2024 fiscal year.

Key Recommendations:

  • Standardize the "Toronto Model": Toronto achieved the highest efficiency score (0.042), outperforming Kelowna and Winnipeg.
  • Liquidate Dead Stock: Immediate clearance required for stagnant Technology and Household inventory.
  • Implement Flex Staffing: Historical data predicts surges in May and September, requiring temporary staffing to handle throughput.

1. Benchmarking Warehouse Efficiency

To determine which facility was performing best, I created a custom Warehouse Efficiency Score. This metric balances shipping volume against total inventory held.

Efficiency = (Total Shipped / Shipping Days) / Total Inventory

Insight: While Kelowna shipped the highest volume, Toronto was the superior performer because it achieved similar results while holding significantly less stock.

Figure 1: Toronto outperforms in efficiency vs. inventory held.

2. Supply vs. Demand Analysis

I identified a significant "Supply-Demand Gap" in specific categories. By comparing Total In Stock vs. Total Sold, I calculated a Sell-Through Rate to identify high-risk assets.

  • Problem: Technology and Household categories have high stock levels but critically low sell-through rates.
  • Opportunity: Media is a strong performer that is currently under-prioritized in inventory planning.

3. Financial Forecasting (2024)

Using a linear trend model based on year-over-year performance (2022-2023), I forecasted revenue streams for the upcoming fiscal year.

Risk Alert

Niche payment methods (ApplePay and AMEX) are projected to reach near-zero usage in 2024.

Growth Spot

While most provinces are flat, British Columbia shows signs of significant sales growth based on historical trends.

Seasonality

Revenue predictably surges in May and September, with deep slumps in February, dictating our staffing strategy.

Strategic Recommendations

1

Rationalize Financial Overheads

Phase out ApplePay and AMEX support to eliminate unnecessary merchant fees and administrative maintenance.

2

Implement Dynamic Resourcing

Move away from static hiring. Onboard temporary staff in April to prepare for the May peak, and reduce hours during the February slump.

3

Optimize Warehouse Operations

Audit Toronto's workflow and implement their staffing and layout procedures at the underperforming Kelowna and Winnipeg locations.
