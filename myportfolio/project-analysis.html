Executive Summary

The objective of this project was to analyze operational data across four key pillars: Warehouse Efficiency, Inventory Health, Financial Trends, and Seasonal Demand. By analyzing 4 years of historical data, I identified critical cost-saving opportunities for the 2024 fiscal year.

Key Recommendations:

  • Standardize the "Toronto Model": Toronto achieved the highest efficiency score (0.042), outperforming Kelowna and Winnipeg.
  • Liquidate Dead Stock: Immediate clearance required for stagnant Technology and Household inventory.
  • Implement Flex Staffing: Historical data predicts surges in May and September, requiring temporary staffing to handle throughput.

1. Benchmarking Warehouse Efficiency

To determine which facility was performing best, I created a custom Warehouse Efficiency Score. This metric balances shipping volume against total inventory held.

Efficiency = (Total Shipped / Shipping Days) / Total Inventory

Insight: While Kelowna shipped the highest volume, Toronto was the superior performer because it achieved similar results while holding significantly less stock.

2. Supply vs. Demand Analysis

I identified a significant "Supply-Demand Gap" in specific categories. By comparing Total In Stock vs. Total Sold, I calculated a Sell-Through Rate to identify high-risk assets.

  • Problem: Technology and Household categories have high stock levels but critically low sell-through rates.
  • Opportunity: Media is a strong performer that is currently under-prioritized in inventory planning.

3. Financial Forecasting (2024)

Using a linear trend model based on year-over-year performance (2022-2023), I forecasted revenue streams for the upcoming fiscal year.

Risk Alert

Niche payment methods (ApplePay and AMEX) are projected to reach near-zero usage in 2024.

Growth Spot

While most provinces are flat, British Columbia shows signs of significant sales growth based on historical trends.

Seasonality

Revenue predictably surges in May and September, with deep slumps in February, dictating our staffing strategy.

Technical Implementation

1. Complex Data Aggregation (SQL)

sql_warehouse_efficiency.sql 
SELECT 
    s.warehouseId, 
    w.warehouseName, 
    SUM(op.quantity) AS totalShipped
FROM shipment s
JOIN warehouse w ON w.warehouseId = s.warehouseId
JOIN ordersummary os ON os.shipmentId = s.shipmentId
JOIN orderproduct op ON op.orderId = os.orderId
GROUP BY s.warehouseId, w.warehouseName
ORDER BY totalShipped DESC;

Context: To calculate the "Efficiency Score," I aggregated data from three distinct tables. This query isolates the Throughput Velocity by joining shipment logs with order details.

2. Excel Logic & Forecasting

Linear Forecasting Model

To predict 2024 revenue, I utilized the FORECAST.LINEAR algorithm based on the absolute year-over-year performance between 2022 and 2023. This model identified that while major payment methods are declining, niche methods are collapsing entirely.

=FORECAST.LINEAR(2024, B2:F2, {2019,2020...})

The "Zero-Floor" Constraint

Challenge: The rapid decline of ApplePay usage caused the linear model to predict negative revenue for 2024, which is a statistical anomaly.

Solution: I applied a logic constraint to floor values at $0. This manually corrects the algorithm to reflect the business reality that a payment method effectively becomes obsolete rather than costing revenue.

Advanced Data Transformation

Identifying "Dead Stock"

SQL: LEFT JOIN

Problem: Standard queries filter out products with zero sales. We needed to specifically identify these non-performers to liquidate them. 

SELECT 
    c.categoryName,
    -- Handle NULLs for items with 0 sales
    COALESCE(SUM(op.quantity), 0) AS totalSold
FROM product p
JOIN category c ON p.categoryId = c.categoryId
JOIN productinventory pi ON p.productId = pi.productId
-- Keep products even if they have no order history
LEFT JOIN orderProduct op 
    ON p.productId = op.productId
GROUP BY c.categoryName
ORDER BY totalInStock DESC;

Used LEFT JOIN to ensure unsold inventory remained in the dataset, and COALESCE to format null values as 0 for accurate calculation.

Customer Segmentation

SQL: Subquery

Problem: Linking orders to payment methods required deduplicating customer records to find their primary card. 

FROM orderSummary o
JOIN shipment s ON o.shipmentId = s.shipmentId
-- Derived table to isolate default payment
JOIN (
    SELECT 
        customerId, 
        MIN(paymentMethodId) AS defaultId
    FROM paymentMethod
    GROUP BY customerId
) customer_card 
ON o.customerId = customer_card.customerId

Utilized a derived table (Subquery) within the JOIN statement to pre-aggregate customer payment data before linking it to transaction history.
